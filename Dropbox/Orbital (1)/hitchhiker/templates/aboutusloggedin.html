About Us



NUS School of Computing’s Orbital Programme is a self-driven programming summer experience for first year students, which focus on self-learning and building something useful. For Orbital 2015, Team Anonymous is introducing HitchHiker, a revolutionary way to carpool! HitchHiker is a user-friendly website for people who wish to carpool in Singapore. We aim to help travellers get to their destinations with greater ease and drivers cut down on their petrol cost. On top of that, HitchHiker allows you to make new friends while doing your part to protect the environment by reducing your carbon footprint!

How Does Hitchhiker Work?



HitchHiker is a user-friendly website that allows users to take up the role of both the offerer and requester. After logging in, users are free to create and edit trips, search for trips that they can join and contact other users to plan the trips together. It does not matter if you are offering a ride or requesting for a ride, HitchHiker has something for you!

If you are offering a ride, scroll over 'My Offers/Requests' and select 'Make New Offer' from the drop down list. Enter all necessary details about the ride that you are offering (you can use Google Maps provided to find your location zip code) and submit the form. Similarly, if you are requesting a ride, choose 'Make New Request' and enter all necessary details before submitting the form.

After making an offer or request, you can search for other people's request or offer by scrolling over 'Received Offers/Requests' and selecting 'Search For Others' Offers' or 'Search For Others' Requests'. Enter the date you want to search for and a list of available offers/requests will show up in the search results. You can then choose the offer/request you want to contact by clicking the 'Contact' button and fill in all necessary details in the form.

Finally, you can check if you have received any offers/requests for your made requests/offers by scrolling over 'Received Offers/Requests' and selecting 'Manage Received Offers' or 'Manage Received Requests'. If you have received any offers or requests, you will be able to visualise the route by choosing 'View Route'. This function helps you decide whether to contact the offerer/requester after seeing the route the offerer will go to pick up and drop off the requester. If you decide to take up the received offer/request, you can contact the other party through the email or number provided to discuss the details.
